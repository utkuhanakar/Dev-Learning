# GitHub Account Security Checklist

Follow these steps to ensure your GitHub account and repositories are 100% secure.

## 1. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) 🛡️
- [ ] **Enable 2FA:** Go to [Account Security](https://github.com/settings/security) and ensure 2FA is active. Use an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator or Authy) or a hardware security key.
- [ ] **Recovery Codes:** Download and save your 2FA recovery codes in a safe, offline location.

## 2. Token and Key Management 🔑
- [ ] **Personal Access Tokens (PAT):** Review your tokens at [Tokens](https://github.com/settings/tokens). **Delete any tokens you no longer need**, including the one provided for this task once we're done.
- [ ] **SSH Keys:** Check your registered SSH keys at [SSH and GPG keys](https://github.com/settings/keys). Delete any keys that you don't recognize.

## 3. Session and Login Security 💻
- [ ] **Active Sessions:** Review [Sessions](https://github.com/settings/sessions) to see where you are logged in. Log out of any sessions that seem suspicious or are on devices you no longer use.
- [ ] **Verified Emails:** Ensure all email addresses associated with your account are verified and secure.

## 4. Repository Security 📦
- [ ] **Secret Scanning:** Ensure secret scanning is enabled for your repositories to prevent accidental leaks of keys or passwords.
- [ ] **Dependabot:** Enable Dependabot alerts to get notified about vulnerable dependencies in your projects.

## 5. Third-Party Access 🔌
- [ ] **Authorized OAuth Apps:** Review apps that have access to your GitHub account at [Applications](https://github.com/settings/installations). Revoke access for any app you no longer use.

---
*Note: Safety is a continuous process. Regularly review these settings to keep your professional presence secure.*
